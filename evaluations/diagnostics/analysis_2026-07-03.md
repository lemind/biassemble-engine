**1. `availability_heuristic`** — 2 paragraphs

- `[Political]` — Example of political fear campaign driving overestimation: vivid imagery of a rare threat presented as imminent, causing people to demand action disproportionate to statistical risk.
- `[Social]` — Example of grief-driven risk overestimation: someone who experienced a vivid personal loss overestimates frequency of that type of event, advocates intensively, brings it into every conversation.

Target scenario: edge_001 (Robert), adv_003 (school safety). Must reach > 0.35 against both stories.

**2. `affect_heuristic`** — 2 paragraphs

- `[Social]` — Example of emotional state completely replacing factual evaluation: person in fear or grief makes decisions based entirely on how something feels rather than what evidence shows.
- `[Political]` — Example of gut-feeling political judgment: endorsing a policy or candidate based on the emotional charge of the rhetoric, not the content.

Target scenarios: adv_003, edge_001. Current max score 0.254 — needs >40% improvement.

**3. `framing_effect`** — 2 paragraphs

- `[Political]` — Example of political framing: same policy described as "protecting families" vs "government overreach" producing opposite reactions in the same person.
- `[Social]` — Example of fear framing: presenting a low-probability risk with vivid emotional language ("your child could be next") causing different risk assessment than neutral statistics.

Target scenarios: adv_001, adv_003. Currently not in top 20 for these stories.

**4. `loss_aversion`** — 2 paragraphs
